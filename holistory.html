There are various beliefs in Hindu culture behind celebrating Holi. Few of those are as below:


1. Holika Dahan by Lord Vishnu (Victory of good over evil)


In ancient time, Hiranyakashipu was a very powerful king of Ahuras. It is considered that his brother was killed by Lord Vishnu. In order to take revange of that, he prayed hard of God and gained boon that, He will neither be killed by human nor by animal, He will neither be killed on earth nor in water or sky, He will neither be killed be killed in day time nor in night time, He will neither be kille indoor not outdoor, He will neither be killed by Astra nor by Sastra.

By gaining this boon and many other powers, he start considering himself invincible and became too arogant that start punushing whosoever didn't agreed to pray him as as God. But his own son, Prahalad, denied accepting him as God and start praying Lord Vishnu. After knowing this, king decided to kill his own son with the help of his sister named Holika. As Holika conisdered immune to fire, she sat in fire with having Prahalad in her lap. WIth the God's grace, her immunity got transferred to Prahalad and she burnt to ashes. Immediately after that, Lord Vishnu appeared in form of Narsimha (half human and half lion), during evening time, took Hiranyakashipu at doorstep and killed him with his nail by placing him on his lap.

Since than, that day is celebrated as a symbol of victory of good over evil.



2. Radha Krishna Milan (Symbol of divine love)


It is considered that Lord Krishna and Radha used to live in Brij, a region in northen India. Krishna is considered to have dark skin color and Radha was having very fair skin color. Both were in love since childhood, But one day Krishna asked his mother, Yashoda, that will Radha marry him even if he is not having skin color as she had. At that time Yashoda put various different color on Krihna's body and asked him to identify skin color. Krishna got his answer and in the joy of that, he colored skin of all his ffiends as well of Radha. That day entire village played with colored powder and there were colors everywhere.

Since than, on that day everyone use to colors others irrespective of known or stranger, friend or enemy, young or old as a symbol of unity and love.
